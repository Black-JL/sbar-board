The scenario

Your task
You are LTC Pat Donovan, Deputy Commander for Administration at Stewart Army Community Hospital (100 beds). The Chief of Staff has tasked you with writing an MFR documenting the 90-day SBAR hand-off pilot on Med-Surg and ICU, and recommending whether to expand it enterprise-wide.

The lab

  1. Gather your data ~5 min

    Read everything in the digital source packet. Then walk to the side table at the front of the room and look at the three printed items there — the 90-day SBAR hand-off pilot scorecard, scribbled Med-Surg huddle notes, and a family letter. Take notes or pictures.

  2. Build the prompt and generate the initial output ~10 min

    Gather all of the inputs you'll need for the prompt:

    • Who you are and what document you're producing
    • The format standard (AR 25-50)
    • The source material you actually want it to use
    • Constraints: don't fabricate, third person, plain language, etc.
    • A voice anchor — the reference MFR in the packet works for this

    Think critically about how to structure them into the best prompt you can write. Then open whatever AI tool you brought and run it.

    Read the output critically. Iterate once. Before you iterate, ask yourself: would you sign this and defend it to the hospital commander?

  3. Pair feedback — one piece ~5 min

    Trade drafts with the person next to you. Read their MFR, then give them one piece of feedback. Just one. Not three, not five.

    The question to ask before you write the feedback:

    What is the single change that would make this MFR more likely to be acted on by the Commander?
  4. Revise and submit ~20 min

    Apply your partner's feedback; revise or regenerate as needed. Then upload your finished MFR — the actual document (PDF or Word), not just the text. Give it a pseudonym so you can find it later.

    ↑ Submit your MFR ▤ Browse all submissions

    You can browse everyone's submissions once your own is in. Trouble uploading? Email your draft to jared_black@baylor.edu.

  5. — 10-minute break —
  6. Mock board — score the finalists ~15 min

    The instructor picks finalists from the submissions and opens voting. On the voting page you'll read each finalist's MFR and score it on six dimensions (1–5) — sounds human, source-anchored, restrained, AR 25-50 + BLUF, compelling, no PII — plus a yes/no: would you forward this to the Commander? The tally goes up on the screen.

    ★ Open the mock-board voting

    Voting opens once the instructor posts the finalists. If it says "not open yet," wait for the go-ahead.

  7. Backwards-engineer the winner ~35 min

    The winner walks the class through the tool they used, the prompt they built, and what they iterated. The class extracts the patterns.
